# Hi, I'm Hana  

## About Me

I'm a **policy researcher and data analyst** passionate about poverty reduction, equity, and inclusive development. I currently work at the **World Bank**, where I contribute to data and research initiatives that support evidence-based policy and private sector development with the Business Ready Team.

I recently completed my **Master of Public Policy (MPP)** at the **University of Virginia (UVA)**, where I specialized in **quantitative methods, data analysis, and development policy**.

My work bridges **rigorous analysis and policy relevance**. I use tools such as **R, Stata, SQL, and ArcGIS** to clean and analyze large datasets and translate complex findings into insights for policymakers, governments, and development partners.

Originally from **Sudan**, I bring a perspective shaped by both the challenges of fragile states and the opportunities of inclusive development. I earned my **B.Sc. in Accounting and Finance** from the **University of Khartoum**, where I developed a strong foundation in economics, financial analysis, and quantitative methods.

Previously, I worked as a **Research Assistant at UVA’s Center for Social Innovation**, contributing to projects on **poverty, urban service delivery, and spatial inequality** in informal settlements in **Cape Town**. My work involved cleaning and merging administrative datasets, conducting econometric analysis in **Stata and R**, and using **ArcGIS** to map disparities in access to basic services.

Across my academic and professional experiences, I focus on using **data-driven analysis to inform policies that promote equity, opportunity, and inclusive growth.**
